# Gateways vs Vercel

**Vercel** excels at **frontend and full-stack deploys** tied to Git, with a global edge, serverless functions, and deep Next.js integration on Vercel’s platform.

**Gateways** targets teams that **run workloads in their own AWS, Azure, or GCP accounts**: VMs, databases, DNS, serverless, and more—**composed on a canvas** with workspaces, environments, and a unified API.

| Topic | Gateways | Vercel |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Where compute runs** | Your cloud accounts | Vercel-managed infrastructure |
| **Best fit** | Multi-service architecture, compliance, existing cloud spend | Fast web app deploys, edge, framework-native DX |
| **Networking & data** | Full VPC-style resources per provider | Platform abstractions (routes, env, KV, etc.) |

Use **Vercel** when your product is a web app and you want zero server ops on a single platform. Use **Gateways** when you need **direct control** over cloud resources across providers and a **shared diagram** of how they connect.
