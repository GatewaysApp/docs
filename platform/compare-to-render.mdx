# Gateways vs Render

**Render** offers **managed services** (web services, databases, cron, static sites) on Render’s infrastructure with simple YAML or dashboard workflows.

**Gateways** connects to **your** AWS, Azure, or GCP and lets you **place, connect, and sync** resources (compute, data, DNS, functions) on a **visual architecture map**, with team workspaces and APIs.

| Topic | Gateways | Render |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Ownership** | Resources live in your cloud billing | Render-hosted stacks |
| **Scope** | Multi-cloud, VPC-level patterns | Single PaaS surface |
| **Operations** | Sync, delete, and automate via unified resources API | Render APIs and dashboards |

Choose **Render** for straightforward **PaaS hosting** with less cloud sprawl. Choose **Gateways** when you **standardize on major clouds** and want one console for **architecture and governance**.
